# Engineering Log: Solving CloudFront OAC Bottlenecks and Refining User Experience

As a CTO, I’ve learned that the most challenging bugs aren't usually in the business logic, but in the "invisible" layers: networking, infrastructure permissions, and the subtle psychology of UI/UX. Today was a prime example of this, as I spent a significant portion of my time navigating AWS CloudFront's quirks and polishing the frontend of the Heritage Radio project.

## 1. Infrastructure: The CloudFront OAC "Accident" and the Proxy Workaround

The most critical technical challenge today involved a failure in the CloudFront Origin Access Control (OAC) for the `/live/*` paths. When OAC doesn't behave as expected, it can lead to intermittent 403 Forbidden errors that are notoriously difficult to debug because they happen at the edge.

**The Technical Decision:**
Rather than spending hours fighting the OAC configuration in a production environment where uptime is paramount, I decided to implement a **backend HLS proxy**. 

By routing requests through `backend/app/routers/hls_proxy.py`, we can use `boto3` to stream audio directly from S3. This effectively bypasses the CloudFront OAC issue entirely. To ensure this doesn't degrade performance, I implemented an **LRU (Least Recently Used) cache** in the `hls_builder` with a 3GB cap.

## 2. Mobile Stability: Exponential Backoff for Network Recovery

In the mobile app (v0.9.84), I encountered a scenario where brief network flickers caused the HLS player to crash or stop playback entirely.

**The Insight:**
Simple retries often lead to "retry storms" that can overwhelm a recovering connection. I implemented **exponential backoff** in `useHlsPlayer.native.ts`. Instead of retrying every second, the app now waits progressively longer between attempts. This creates a much smoother "automatic recovery" experience for the user, making the app feel resilient rather than fragile.

## 3. UX Polish: The "Next Action" Icon Pattern

On the frontend, I noticed a discrepancy in how the theme toggles were behaving. In some parts of the site, the icon represented the *current state* (e.g., showing a sun when it's light mode), while in others, it represented the *action* (e.g., showing a moon to indicate "switch to dark mode").

**The Fix:**
I unified all toggles to the **"Next Action"** pattern. 
- **Light Mode $\rightarrow$ Moon Icon** (Click to go Dark)
- **Dark Mode $\rightarrow$ Sun Icon** (Click to go Light)

It seems like a small detail, but consistency in these micro-interactions is what separates a professional product from a prototype. As seen in the diff for `Gnb.tsx`:

```tsx
- {theme === "light" ? <SunIcon /> : <MoonIcon />}
+ {theme === "light" ? <MoonIcon /> : <SunIcon />}
```

## Summary of Changes

- **Infra:** Upgraded RDS instance (`db.t4g.small` $\rightarrow$ `medium`) to handle increased load and updated CDK stacks for better WAF/CloudFront secret integration.
- **Backend:** Added HLS proxy to bypass OAC issues and implemented a 3GB LRU cache for audio segments.
- **Mobile:** v0.9.84 release with exponential backoff for `PlaybackError` and a cache-buster fix for cycle transitions.
- **Frontend:** Unified theme toggle logic across GNB and footer.

Today was a reminder that engineering is often a balance between the "perfect" architectural solution and the "practical" one that keeps the service running. At CPLABS, we prioritize stability and user experience, and today's changes reflect that philosophy.
