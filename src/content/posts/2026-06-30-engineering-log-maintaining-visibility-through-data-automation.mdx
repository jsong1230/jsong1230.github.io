# Engineering Log: Maintaining Visibility through Data Automation

As a CTO and engineer with over two decades of experience, I’ve learned that the most overlooked part of a high-performing development pipeline isn't the code itself, but the **visibility** of the progress. In a fast-paced environment—whether leading teams at CPLABS or managing personal open-source projects—knowing exactly where the momentum is shifting across multiple repositories is crucial for strategic decision-making.

Today's work was a focused exercise in updating my personal development dashboard. While it may look like a simple JSON update on the surface, it represents a larger philosophy of "Quantified Engineering."

### The Technical Core: Data-Driven Progress

The primary task today was updating `src/data/dashboard.json`. This file serves as the backend for my activity tracking, aggregating commit frequencies and last-activity timestamps across various projects.

Looking at the diff, there is a significant update regarding the `metadium_homepage` repository:

```json
+ {
+   "name": "metadium_homepage",
+   "last_date": "2026-06-29",
+   "last_msg": "배포 인프라(Docker/Caddy용) + SEO 추가 (챗봇 제외)",
+   "branch": "master",
+   "activity": [0, 0, 0, 0, 0, 23, 0],
+   "commits_7d": 39,
+   "commits_30d": 39
+ }
```

The spike in activity (23 commits in a single window) highlights a critical phase of the project: transitioning from raw development to production-ready infrastructure. The focus here was on Dockerization and Caddy configuration—essential steps for ensuring a stable, scalable deployment.

### Technical Decisions & Insights

When managing multiple repositories—ranging from blockchain-related projects to AI agents like `claude-agent`—I've made a conscious decision to automate the "Recap" process. 

**1. The Shift from Weekly to Daily Granularity**
In the diff, you'll notice a change in the `jsong1230.github.io` commit message:
- *Old:* `"Auto-generate weekly recap"`
- *New:* `"Auto-generate daily posts"`

This shift reflects a move toward a tighter feedback loop. In the world of Web3 and AI, a week is an eternity. By moving to daily automated summaries, I can maintain a more precise audit trail of technical pivots and incremental wins.

**2. Infrastructure over Features**
The `metadium_homepage` update emphasizes SEO and deployment infrastructure. Many developers make the mistake of focusing solely on features. However, my experience at CPLABS taught me that a feature is only as good as its accessibility. Without a robust Caddy/Docker setup and a solid SEO foundation, the most sophisticated code remains invisible to the world.

### Final Thoughts

Updating a JSON file might seem like a "chore" (as labeled in the commit), but for a CTO, these metrics are a pulse check. Seeing the activity shift from `heritageradio` toward `metadium_homepage` tells me that my current priority is correctly aligned with the project's lifecycle: moving from the "build" phase to the "optimize and deploy" phase.

Engineering is not just about writing lines of code; it is about managing the flow of value. By keeping a rigorous, data-backed dashboard of my work, I ensure that no project stagnates and that every technical decision is tracked and justified.
